Changu Narayan Temple

Changu Narayan Temple, situated near Bhaktapur in Nepal, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the oldest Hindu temples in the Kathmandu Valley. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, it dates back to the 4th century and showcases exceptional architecture with intricate wood and stone carvings. The temple complex, perched atop a hill surrounded by verdant scenery, features a rich collection of ancient inscriptions, statues, and sculptures that reflect Nepal's artistic heritage. Revered both spiritually and historically, Changu Narayan remains a significant cultural and religious landmark, drawing pilgrims and tourists alike.
