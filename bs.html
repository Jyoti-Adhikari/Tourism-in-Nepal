Boudhanath Stupa

Boudhanath Stupa, located in Kathmandu, Nepal, is one of the largest and most significant Buddhist stupas in the world. Constructed in the 14th century, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a key pilgrimage destination for Tibetan Buddhists. The stupa's massive mandala design is a powerful symbol of the Buddha's mind, and it is adorned with prayer flags and the all-seeing eyes of Buddha painted on its tower, representing wisdom and compassion. Surrounded by vibrant markets and Tibetan monasteries, Boudhanath serves as a central hub for Buddhist culture and meditation practices.
