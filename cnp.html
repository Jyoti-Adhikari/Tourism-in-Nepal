Chitwan National Park

Chitwan National Park, located in south-central Nepal, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its rich biodiversity and conservation efforts. Established in 1973, it spans 952.63 square kilometers and features a diverse range of ecosystems, including subtropical forests, grasslands, and riverine areas. The park is home to an array of wildlife, notably the endangered one-horned rhinoceros, Bengal tiger, and Gharial crocodile. Visitors can explore the park through guided jungle safaris, canoe rides, and bird-watching tours, experiencing its lush landscapes and diverse fauna firsthand.
