Lumbini

Lumbini, situated in the Rupandehi District of Nepal, is a significant pilgrimage site as the birthplace of Siddhartha Gautama, who later became known as the Buddha. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Lumbini features the sacred Maya Devi Temple, marking the exact spot of Buddha's birth. The site also includes ancient ruins, monasteries, and the Ashoka Pillar, erected by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE. Lumbini is a center of spiritual significance, attracting Buddhists and visitors from around the world who seek to connect with the origins of Buddhism and explore its serene and contemplative atmosphere.
